Self-Study

In addition to my formal education, I am deeply committed to self-study in the field of cybersecurity.
I believe that continuous learning is essential in this rapidly evolving industry, and I actively seek out resources and opportunities to expand my knowledge.

My self-study efforts include:

  • Completing modules and rooms in HackTheBox and TryHackMe
  • Participating in SWIFT competitions and online competitions like Hivestorm
  • Practicing defense strategies in my own virtual machines

Through these activities, I aim to stay current with the latest trends, tools, and techniques in cybersecurity, enhancing my skills and preparing myself for a successful career in the field.

HackTheBox

HackTheBox is an online platform that allows individuals to practice and improve their penetration testing skills in a legal and controlled environment.
It offers a wide range of challenges and labs that simulate real-world scenarios, providing hands-on experience in identifying and exploiting vulnerabilities.

By engaging with HackTheBox, I have been able to enhance my technical skills, learn new tools and techniques, and gain a deeper understanding of cybersecurity concepts.
This platform has been instrumental in my self-study journey, allowing me to apply theoretical knowledge in practical scenarios.

TryHackMe

TryHackMe is another online platform that provides interactive cybersecurity training through hands-on labs and challenges.
It caters to individuals of all skill levels, from beginners to advanced practitioners, offering a structured learning path in various cybersecurity domains.

TryHackMe is very similar to HackTheBox, but it focuses more on guided learning paths and educational content.

Competitions

Participating in cybersecurity competitions is a great way to apply my skills in a competitive environment and learn from others in the field.
I have taken part in various competitions, including ones hosted by SWIFT like RvB, PvP, and SnD, and external competitions like Hivestorm.
These competitions challenge me to think critically, solve complex problems, and work under pressure, all of which are essential skills for a cybersecurity professional.

Virtual Machine Environments

In addition to online platforms and competitions, I also practice defense strategies in my own virtual machine environments.
By setting up and configuring virtual machines, I can simulate real-world scenarios and test various security measures to protect against potential threats.
This hands-on approach allows me to experiment with different tools and techniques, gaining practical experience in securing systems and networks.

Using VirtualBox and VMWare, I have installed operating systems such as Kali Linux, Ubuntu, and Windows Server to create a diverse range of environments for testing and learning.
